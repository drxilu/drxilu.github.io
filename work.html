Current focus

Sloths Intel

My current focus is founding and building Sloths Intel — an independent practice and company dedicated to developing practical, ethical AI and data systems.

The work centres on translating research-level methods into tools that operate reliably under real-world constraints, particularly for small organisations and resource-limited teams.

Through Sloths Intel, I explore how deliberate system design, lightweight analytics, and transparent modelling can support better decision-making, while remaining accountable, evaluable, and grounded in real use.

Selected Projects

Genomic Surveillance & Decision Support for Public Health

Context. Large-scale genomic surveillance generates complex, high-volume data that must be translated into actionable public-health insight.

My role. As a Research Fellow and Research Associate, I designed and implemented machine-learning and Bayesian modelling frameworks to analyse the population dynamics of Streptococcus pneumoniae across national and global datasets.

What was built. Machine-learning pipelines for sub-lineage clustering, Bayesian models for forecasting disease burden and transmission, automated workflows using reproducible pipelines, and visualisation tools for interdisciplinary teams.

Outcome. Improved resolution of population structure and transmission patterns, evidence to support vaccine strategy and epidemiological planning, and multiple conference presentations with ongoing publications.

Why it matters. This work demonstrates my ability to turn complex, noisy data into reliable decision support under real-world constraints — a core principle behind my current work and the founding of Sloths Intel.

Scalable Data Curation & Workflow Automation

Context. Public and international health projects often rely on heterogeneous datasets collected across countries, standards, and infrastructures.

My role. I curated and harmonised large genomic datasets (N > 20,000) and supported the migration and automation of data workflows in collaborative research environments.

What was built. Data cleaning and validation pipelines, metadata harmonisation processes, automated workflows to support downstream analysis, and documentation enabling reuse by technical and non-technical teams.

Outcome. Clean, standardised datasets ready for population-scale analysis, reduced manual effort and error in downstream research, and improved collaboration between academic and technical teams.

Why it matters. This project reflects my focus on deliberate system design — building tools that reduce friction and support sustainable, repeatable work.

Predictive Modelling for Policy-Relevant Decisions

Context. Policy and planning decisions require models that are not only accurate, but also explainable and trustworthy.

My role. I developed predictive models to estimate disease burden, antimicrobial resistance, and future epidemiological trends across multiple countries.

What was built. Bayesian inference frameworks for population modelling, scenario-based forecasting tools, and evaluation methods to assess uncertainty and robustness.

Outcome. Predictions used to inform public-health discussions and planning, alongside the supervision of postgraduate student projects and method transfer across international datasets.

Why it matters. This work underpins my emphasis on transparent, evaluable modelling — a key value I carry into my independent and company-based work.

Reliable Systems Design in Next-Generation Networks

Context. Next-generation communication systems must operate reliably in shared and constrained environments.

My role. During my PhD and subsequent research, I designed theoretical and applied frameworks for coexistence between licensed and unlicensed wireless systems.

What was built. Game-theoretic and systems-level models, performance evaluation frameworks, and peer-reviewed research published in IEEE journals.

Outcome. Widely cited academic publications and contributions to understanding reliability and coexistence in complex technical systems.

Why it matters. Although technically distinct, this work shaped my approach to robust decision-making under uncertainty, which continues to influence my applied AI and data systems work today.
